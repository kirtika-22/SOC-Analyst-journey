# Day 03 - Interview Questions

## Q1. What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from cyber threats and unauthorized access.

---

## Q2. What is a Security Operations Center (SOC)?

A Security Operations Center (SOC) is a centralized team responsible for monitoring, detecting, investigating, and responding to cybersecurity incidents.

---

## Q3. What is the difference between Threat, Vulnerability, and Risk?

Threat:
Anything capable of causing damage.

Vulnerability:
A weakness in a system.

Risk:
The possibility that a threat exploits a vulnerability.

---

## Q4. Explain the CIA Triad.

Confidentiality – Protects data from unauthorized access.

Integrity – Ensures data accuracy.

Availability – Ensures systems remain accessible.

---

## Q5. Name five Cybersecurity Domains.

- Network Security
- Endpoint Security
- Cloud Security
- Application Security
- Security Operations (SOC)

---

## Q6. What is SIEM?

SIEM stands for Security Information and Event Management. It collects, correlates, and analyzes logs from multiple sources to detect security incidents.

---

## Q7. Name some SIEM tools.

- Splunk
- Microsoft Sentinel
- QRadar
- Wazuh

---

## Q8. What are common log sources in SOC?

- Windows Event Logs
- Linux Logs
- Firewall Logs
- DNS Logs
- VPN Logs

---

## Q9. What is Alert Triage?

Alert triage is the process of validating, prioritizing, investigating, and escalating security alerts.

---

## Q10. Explain the Cyber Kill Chain.

The Cyber Kill Chain consists of:

1. Reconnaissance
2. Weaponization
3. Delivery
4. Exploitation
5. Installation
6. Command and Control
7. Actions on Objectives

---

## Q11. What is Defense in Depth?

Defense in Depth is a layered security approach where multiple security controls are implemented to protect systems.

---

## Q12. What is the role of a SOC Analyst?

A SOC Analyst monitors alerts, investigates suspicious activities, analyzes logs, responds to incidents, and documents findings.

---

## Q13. What is the purpose of endpoint security?

Endpoint security protects devices such as laptops, desktops, and servers from cyber threats.

---

## Q14. Why are logs important in cybersecurity?

Logs provide evidence of system activities and help analysts detect, investigate, and respond to security incidents.

---

## Q15. What are some cybersecurity best practices?

- Use strong passwords
- Enable MFA
- Keep systems updated
- Perform regular backups
- Monitor logs
- Conduct security awareness training
