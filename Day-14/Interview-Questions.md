# Day 14 - SIEM & Wazuh Interview Questions with Answers

## 1. What is SIEM?

Answer:
SIEM (Security Information and Event Management) is a security solution that collects logs from different devices, normalizes them, correlates events, detects suspicious activities, and generates alerts and reports.

---

## 2. Why do organizations use SIEM?

Answer:
Organizations use SIEM because:

- Centralized log management
- Detect cyber attacks
- Real-time monitoring
- Faster incident response
- Compliance reporting
- Forensic investigation

---

## 3. What are logs?

Answer:
Logs are records of events generated by operating systems, applications, firewalls, routers, IDS/IPS, antivirus, cloud services, and many other devices.

Example:
- Login Success
- Login Failure
- File Deleted
- User Created

---

## 5. What are SIEM core functions?

Answer:

1. Data Collection
2. Log Normalization
3. Event Correlation
4. Alerting
5. Reporting
6. Incident Investigation

---

## 6. What is Log Normalization?

Answer:
Normalization converts logs from different devices into a common format so that the SIEM can analyze them easily.

---

## 7. What is Event Correlation?

Answer:
Correlation combines multiple log events to identify suspicious behavior that may indicate an attack.

Example:
- Multiple failed logins
- Successful login afterwards

SIEM generates a Brute Force alert.

---

## 8. What is Alerting in SIEM?

Answer:
Alerting is the process of notifying SOC analysts whenever suspicious activities are detected.

---

## 9. What is Compliance Reporting?

Answer:
Compliance reports help organizations meet standards like:

- PCI-DSS
- HIPAA
- ISO 27001
- GDPR

---

## 10. What is Digital Forensics?

Answer:
Digital Forensics is the investigation of security incidents using logs and evidence collected by SIEM.

---

## 11. What attacks can SIEM detect?

Answer:

- Brute Force attacks
- Malware infections
- Insider threats
- Data exfiltration
- Privilege escalation
- Suspicious logins

---

## 12. What is Wazuh?

Answer:
Wazuh is an open-source SIEM and XDR platform used for log collection, monitoring, threat detection, vulnerability detection, file integrity monitoring, and compliance.

---

## 13. What are the components of Wazuh?

Answer:

- Wazuh Agent
- Wazuh Manager
- Wazuh Indexer
- Wazuh Dashboard

---

## 14. Difference between Wazuh Agent and Manager?

Answer:

Agent:
Installed on endpoints and sends logs.

Manager:
Receives logs, analyzes them, applies rules, and generates alerts.

---

## 15. Which services should be running after installing Wazuh?

Answer:

- wazuh-manager
- wazuh-dashboard
- wazuh-indexer

Verify using:

sudo systemctl status wazuh-manager

sudo systemctl status wazuh-dashboard

sudo systemctl status wazuh-indexer

---

## 21. What is the difference between Log Management and SIEM?

Answer:

Log Management stores and searches logs.

SIEM stores logs and also analyzes, correlates, detects threats, and generates alerts.

## 23. Give an example of SIEM detecting a brute-force attack.

Answer:
If a user fails to log in 10 times and then succeeds, SIEM correlates these events and generates a Brute Force alert.

---

## 24. Why is SIEM important in SOC?

Answer:
SIEM acts as the brain of the SOC by collecting logs, detecting attacks, generating alerts, supporting investigations, and helping analysts respond quickly.

---

## 25. What command enables the Wazuh Agent service?

Answer:

sudo systemctl start wazuh-agent
